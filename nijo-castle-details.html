Nijo Castle

4.7 (600)

Nijo Castle (二条城, Nijō-jō) is a historic castle located in Kyoto, Japan, originally constructed in 1603 as the residence of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the first shogun of the Edo Period. Renowned for its beautiful gardens and exquisite architecture, the castle complex features a series of buildings, including the Ninomaru Palace, known for its stunning sliding doors and "nightingale floors" designed to chirp upon walking, serving as a security measure against intruders. ​Nijo Castle is a UNESCO World Heritage site that represents the political power of the shogunate and is celebrated for its cultural significance, showcasing the artistic grandeur of Japan's feudal era​. The spacious grounds also contain meticulously designed gardens, ideal for leisurely strolls, making it a popular destination for both tourists and locals alike.

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: UNESCO World Heritage Site, exquisite architecture, historical significance, beautiful gardens.
  2. Duration: Year-round, with peak visitors during cherry blossom season and fall foliage.
  • Ninomaru Palace: Explore the ornate interiors and nightingale floors.
  • Ninomaru Garden: Enjoy the serene garden's manicured landscapes and ponds.
  • ​Historical Exhibits: Learn about the castle's Edo and Meiji period history.​
  • Seasonal Festivals: Attend spring festivals during cherry blossom season.
  • Nearby Sites: Visit the Kyoto Imperial Palace and nearby charming streets.
  • Visit Early: Arrive around 8:45 AM for quieter exploration and better photos.​
  • Check Hours: Verify opening hours and seasonal changes before visiting.
  • Join a Tour: Take a guided tour for insights into the castle's history.
  • Wear Comfortably: Use comfortable footwear for exploring the expansive grounds.
  • Bring Cash: Carry cash, as card acceptance may be limited for small purchases.
