Location: Arashiyama District, Ukyo Ward, Kyoto City

Yume Kyoto Kimono & Yukata Dressing

4.9 (300)

Yume Kyoto offers a wide range of kimonos and yukatas for both men and women, with options for seasonal and traditional designs. The staff provides expert assistance in choosing and dressing, ensuring a comfortable and authentic experience. They also offer hairstyling services to complete the look. After selecting an option and getting dressed you can walk around Togetsukyo, Daikaku temple and many more places around Arashiyama but be sure to return the Kimono on that same day by 6 o'clock in the evening!

Tips

  1. Seasonal Availability: Wearing a kimono or yukata is a quintessential Japanese cultural experience. Kimono are traditionally worn for formal occasions, while yukata are lighter and suited for summer or casual outings.
  2. Duration: Yukata: Typically available in warmer months (May–September). Kimono: Offered year-round, with designs reflecting the seasons (e.g., cherry blossoms in spring).
  3. Accessibility: Dressing locations like Yume Kyoto are easily accessible by public transport, near Kyoto’s top landmarks.
  • Photo Opportunities: Yukata: Typically available in warmer months (May–September). Kimono: Offered year-round, with designs reflecting the seasons (e.g., cherry blossoms in spring).
  • Stroll through Gion or Higashiyama, iconic districts with traditional architecture. Visit Kiyomizu-dera for stunning temple shots.
  • Tea Ceremony: Participate in a tea ceremony at a nearby tea house for an immersive cultural experience.
  • Dining in Style: Enjoy kaiseki (traditional multi-course Japanese meal) or Kyoto sweets at specialty cafes, like those near Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka.
  • Festivals: If visiting during seasonal events, wear a yukata to fit in at festivals like the Gion Matsuri in summer.
  • Timing: Arrive early in the day to make the most of your rental and avoid crowds.
  • Plan for at least 30 minutes for dressing and hairstyling.
  • Clothing Prep: Wear light, fitted undergarments for comfort.
  • Avoid bulky or brightly colored clothing that may show under the kimono/yukata.
  • Shoes: Rental shops provide geta (traditional wooden sandals), but wear socks if it's chilly.
  • Weather Check: On rainy days, many rental shops provide umbrellas or raincoats.
  • Return Policy: Rentals are typically for the day, so plan your activities to allow time to return the outfit by the shop’s closing hours.
