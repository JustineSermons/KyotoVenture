Kyoto Station

5.0 (200)

​Kyōto Station is a major railway station and transportation hub located in Kyōto, Japan.​ It features Japan’s second-largest station building, which incorporates a shopping mall, hotel, movie theater, and various local government facilities all under one roof. Opened in 1997 to commemorate Kyoto's 1,200th anniversary, the station showcases a modern architectural design with a futuristic feel, attracting both travelers and tourists alike.

Details

Interest/Topic: Festivals & Events

Popularity: Very High

Reservation: Not Required

Estimated Price Range: Free to enter; train tickets vary widely, starting from around ¥200 ($1.50) for local trips to several thousand yen for long-distance services.

Extra Info

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: Architectural marvel, transportation hub, shopping and dining options, sightseeing access.
  2. Duration: Year-round, with particular buzz during peak travel seasons and festivals.
  • Modern Design: Kyoto Station features an impressive architectural design, merging functionality with aesthetic appeal.
  • Transportation Gateway: It serves as the central hub for shinkansen and local trains, facilitating easy travel across Japan.
  • Shopping and Dining: Enjoy various restaurants and shops within the station complex, making it a vibrant destination.
  • Plan Arrival: Utilize the tourist information center for guidance on local attractions and travel options.
  • Explore the Station: Take time to appreciate the intricate design and viewpoints within the station.
  • Check Schedules: Stay updated on train timings for efficient travel planning.
