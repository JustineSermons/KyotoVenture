Uji, located between Kyoto and Nara, is a picturesque city known for its rich history, serene riverside scenery, and world-famous green tea. This charming destination offers visitors a perfect blend of cultural landmarks and culinary delights, making it a must-visit spot for anyone exploring the Kansai region.

  1. Stroll Along the Uji River: Enjoy a peaceful walk along the riverbank, lined with cherry blossoms in spring and vibrant foliage in autumn. Consider a traditional cormorant fishing experience during the summer evenings.
  2. Sip and Savor Uji Matcha: Explore tea shops and cafes offering matcha treats like ice cream, soba noodles, and desserts. Many establishments also provide tea-tasting experiences.
  3. Discover Ujigami Shrine: The oldest existing Shinto shrine in Japan, this sacred site is a serene spot to learn about Japan's spiritual history.
  4. Explore the Tale of Genji Museum: Dive into the world of Japan's classic literary masterpiece and learn about the influence of Uji on this timeless tale.
