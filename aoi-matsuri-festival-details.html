Aoi Matsuri Festival

4.7 (150)

The Aoi Matsuri (Hollyhock Festival) is one of Kyoto's three major festivals, held on May 15th each year. It features a grand procession from the Kamo Shrines to the Imperial Palace, with participants dressed in Heian-era costumes, carrying hollyhock leaves as symbols of divine protection. The festival, which dates back over a thousand years, includes rituals, traditional music, and performances, offering a glimpse into Japan's ancient imperial culture. It is a solemn and visually striking event, celebrating peace and prosperity.

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: A grand procession of 500 participants in Heian-era attire, led by the Saio-dai, featuring ox-drawn carriages, horses, and hollyhock-decorated rituals at Shimogamo and Kamigamo Shrines.
  2. Duration: About 4–5 hours on May 15, from morning to early afternoon. Parade begins at 10:30AM at the southern gate of the Imperial Palace and ends around 3:30PM.
  3. Annual festival every May 15
  • Watch the Main Procession (Roto no Gi)
  • Experience the ritual ceremonies
  • Take photos of Heian-Era clothes
  • Sample street food and local cuisine
  • Explore the historic shrines
  • Enjoy traditional performances
  • Arrive early for the Procession
  • Reserve seats for premium views
  • Plan your route
  • Respect the atmosphere
  • Check the weather in case of rain.
  • Wear comfortable shoes since you may have to walk or stand for extended periods.
