Location: Nakagyo Ward, Kyoto City

Kyoto Manga Museum

4.8 (400)

The Kyoto International Manga Museum is a dedicated space celebrating the art and culture of manga, located in the Nakagyō-ku district of Kyoto, Japan. ​Opened in 2006, the museum houses a vast collection of approximately 300,000 manga volumes, making it one of the largest manga museums in the world.​ Visitors can freely browse and read from a selection of over 50,000 manga titles displayed on shelves, providing an immersive experience into the diverse world of Japanese comic art. The museum also features exhibitions, workshops, and events related to manga and its history, appealing to both avid fans and newcomers alike.

Details

Interest/Topic: Anime & Pop Culture

Popularity: High

Reservation: Not Required

Estimated Price Range: 900 yen ($8) for adults; discounts available for students and younger visitors

Extra Info

Things to Do

Tips

  1. Highlights: Extensive manga collection, cultural exhibitions, reading area, community events.
  2. Duration: Year-round, with special exhibitions and events held throughout the year.
  3. Annual festival Gion Matsuri (Gion Festival) every July.
  • Explore Collection: Access 300,000 manga volumes, including international comics.​
  • Reading Area: Browse and read diverse manga in a spacious setting.
  • Exhibitions: Engage with rotating displays on manga history and genres.
  • Cultural Events: Attend workshops and lectures promoting manga culture.
  • Global Impact: Learn about manga's influence on art and culture worldwide.
  • ​Arrive Early: Get to the museum around 10 AM to avoid crowds.​
  • Check Fees: Know entrance fees for adults and children, including special event costs.
  • Bring Cash: Some vendors may only accept cash, so have yen ready.
  • Allow Time: Plan for at least two hours to explore the museum comfortably.
  • Respect Space: Maintain a quiet atmosphere in the reading area for all visitors.
